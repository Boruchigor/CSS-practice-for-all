Lüneburg - small and cozy

It is always a good idea to escape a big city, aca Hamburg, and delve into medieval streets of Lüneburg.

Just half an hour’s drive from Hamburg, easily accessible by train or car via the A 250, you will find Lüneburg, a Hanseatic City dating back from medieval times. It was Lüneburg that gave its name to the heath nearby. In the Middle Ages it was one of the richest towns in the north of Germany: its salt mines made it a busy centre of trade, wealth and power. Today day-trippers are fascinated by the magnificent patrician houses with their brick gables or the medieval town hall on the market square. This university town offers visitors a lively cultural scene and the greatest number of bars and cafes per mile in Europe.

How to get to Lüneburg from Hamburg:

  1. Go to Hamburg HBf
  2. Buy a return-ticket for "5 Ringe"
  3. Take a yellow Metronom in the direction Lüneburg / Uelzen
  4. Get out in 40-50 minutes (depends on a number of stops)
